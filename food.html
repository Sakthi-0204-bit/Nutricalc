Food Descriptions

Flours

Wheat Flour: 364 calories per 100g. Wheat flour is made from grinding wheat grains and is a staple in many cooking and baking recipes.

Whole Wheat Flour: 340 calories per 100g. This flour is made by grinding whole wheat kernels and retains more nutrients compared to white flour.

Maida (Refined Wheat Flour): 364 calories per 100g. Maida is a refined, bleached form of wheat flour often used for making pastries and other soft baked goods.

Rice Flour: 366 calories per 100g. Rice flour is made from ground rice and is often used in gluten-free baking and as a thickening agent.

Corn Flour: 365 calories per 100g. Corn flour is finely ground cornmeal and is used in thickening sauces and making gluten-free foods.

Gram Flour: 387 calories per 100g. Made from ground chickpeas, gram flour is used in savory dishes and to make batter for frying foods.

Sorghum Flour: 340 calories per 100g. This gluten-free flour is made from sorghum grain and is often used in gluten-free baking.

Millet Flour: 378 calories per 100g. Millet flour is made from ground millet seeds and is rich in vitamins and minerals.

Oat Flour: 389 calories per 100g. Oat flour is ground from oats and is a popular gluten-free option in baking.

Almond Flour: 575 calories per 100g. Made from ground almonds, this flour is used in gluten-free and low-carb recipes.

Oils

Olive Oil: 884 calories per 100g. Olive oil is widely used for cooking and as a dressing, and is known for its health benefits.

Coconut Oil: 862 calories per 100g. Extracted from the meat of coconuts, coconut oil is commonly used for frying and baking.

Sunflower Oil: 884 calories per 100g. A versatile oil used in cooking, known for its high smoking point and light flavor.

Mustard Oil: 884 calories per 100g. Popular in Indian cuisine, mustard oil is used for frying and tempering.

Ghee: 900 calories per 100g. Ghee is clarified butter, used extensively in Indian and Middle Eastern cooking.

Sesame Oil: 884 calories per 100g. This oil is often used in Asian cuisines, known for its strong, nutty flavor.

Spices

Cumin Seeds: 375 calories per 100g. Cumin adds a warm, earthy flavor to many dishes, especially in Indian and Middle Eastern cuisines.

Coriander: 298 calories per 100g. Coriander is used both as a spice and as fresh leaves (cilantro), known for its citrusy flavor.

Turmeric: 312 calories per 100g. Known for its vibrant yellow color, turmeric is widely used for its flavor and health benefits.

Black Pepper: 255 calories per 100g. Black pepper is a common spice used to add heat and flavor to dishes.

Red Chili Powder: 282 calories per 100g. A key ingredient in many spicy dishes, red chili powder adds heat and color.

Garam Masala: 300 calories per 100g. A blend of ground spices used in Indian cuisine, known for its aromatic and warm flavors.

Mustard Seeds: 508 calories per 100g. Mustard seeds are often used in tempering and for making mustard sauces.

Cinnamon: 247 calories per 100g. A sweet spice used in baking and cooking, known for its aromatic and warm flavor.

Cardamom: 311 calories per 100g. Known for its sweet and aromatic flavor, cardamom is used in both sweet and savory dishes.

Cloves: 274 calories per 100g. Cloves have a strong, aromatic flavor and are used in both savory and sweet dishes.

Asafoetida: 291 calories per 100g. Often used in small amounts for its pungent aroma, it adds depth to Indian dishes.

Masalas

Chaat Masala: 375 calories per 100g. A tangy and spicy mixture used in Indian street food.

Pav Bhaji Masala: 350 calories per 100g. A spice mix used to prepare pav bhaji, a popular street food in India.

Meats

Chicken: 239 calories per 100g. Chicken is a lean meat, high in protein and commonly used in various dishes.

Beef: 250 calories per 100g. Beef is a rich source of protein, iron, and essential vitamins.

Mutton: 294 calories per 100g. Mutton is the meat from an adult sheep and is commonly used in curries and stews.

Pork: 242 calories per 100g. Pork is high in protein and fat, commonly used in cooking worldwide.

Fish: 206 calories per 100g. Fish is a high-quality protein, rich in omega-3 fatty acids.

Fruits

Banana: 89 calories per 100g. Bananas are rich in potassium, vitamins, and provide a quick energy boost.

Apple: 52 calories per 100g. Apples are a good source of fiber and vitamin C.

Orange: 47 calories per 100g. Oranges are rich in vitamin C and provide a refreshing citrus flavor.

Grape: 69 calories per 100g. Grapes are rich in antioxidants and are a popular fruit for snacking.
